Install Healthy Heart

Cardiac health tracker — for patients, and the providers who care for them.

Healthy Heart is a self-contained cardiac health tracking app. It records blood pressure, pulse, oxygen saturation, weight, medications, symptoms, and cardiac events. Every reading is classified by American Heart Association standards with clear color coding. The app generates a clinical report — with trend charts, summary statistics, and medication adherence data — that patients can share with their healthcare provider.

Open Healthy Heart

What to expect on first launch

When you first open the app, you'll see a brief setup interview — name, device types, what you want to track, medication preferences. It takes under a minute.

If you'd rather skip the interview and see the app immediately, tap Preserve existing setup on the first screen.

Demo Mode is on by default. You'll see 30 days of realistic sample data from a hypothetical 58-year-old patient named Alex — elevated blood pressure, an ER visit, medication adjustments, and a recovery arc. All demo data is fictional. Tap ✕ Exit Demo at the top of the screen when you're ready to log your own readings.

How to Install on Your Device

Healthy Heart is a web app that installs directly from your browser. No app store download required. Once installed, it works offline for viewing and logging data; an internet connection is needed only for the Help Bot feature and for the first-time install.

iPhone or iPad (Safari)

  1. Open the Open Healthy Heart link above in Safari — not Chrome, Firefox, or another browser.
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up out of it). It's at the bottom-center of the screen on iPhone; top-right on iPad.
  3. Scroll down in the share sheet and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. You can edit the name if you'd like. Tap Add in the top-right corner.
  5. The app icon (a stylized blood-pressure monitor card) will appear on your home screen. Tap it to open Healthy Heart anytime.

Android (Chrome)

  1. Tap the Open Healthy Heart link above to open it in Chrome.
  2. If Chrome shows an Install or Add to Home Screen prompt at the bottom, tap it.
  3. If no prompt appears: tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner, then tap Install app or Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Install to confirm.
  5. The app icon will appear on your home screen. Tap it to open Healthy Heart anytime.

Desktop (Windows, Mac, Chromebook, Linux)

  1. Open the Open Healthy Heart link above in Chrome, Edge, or another modern Chromium-based browser. Firefox and Safari desktop do not currently support installing progressive web apps.
  2. Look for an install icon in the address bar — typically a small monitor with a downward arrow, or a circular +.
  3. Click it and confirm the installation.
  4. The app will appear in your system's app launcher, Start menu, or Applications folder, and can be pinned to the dock or taskbar.

How to Uninstall

Uninstalling Healthy Heart removes the app from your device along with all of the data you have logged in it — blood-pressure readings, medications, events, settings, and custom sounds. If you want to keep any of that data, use the Export CSV button in the app's Settings tab first.

iPhone or iPad

  1. Long-press the Healthy Heart icon on your home screen.
  2. Tap Remove App, then tap Delete App.
  3. To also clear the browser's stored data: open the iOS Settings app → scroll down and tap Safari → Advanced → Website Data → find abmeapps.github.io and swipe left to delete.

Android

  1. Long-press the Healthy Heart icon on your home screen.
  2. Tap Uninstall, or drag the icon to the trash.
  3. To also clear stored data: open Chrome → three-dot menu → Settings → Site Settings → All Sites → find abmeapps.github.io → Clear & Reset.

Desktop

  1. Open the installed Healthy Heart app.
  2. Click the three-dot menu in the app's title bar and select Uninstall Healthy Heart.
  3. If prompted, check Also clear data, then confirm.
Where your data lives. All of your health information — blood pressure readings, medications, symptoms, events, weight, SpO₂, stethoscope cross-checks — is stored locally on your device. None of it is sent to any server. The one exception is the built-in Help Bot: questions you type into Help Bot are sent to an AI service to generate a response, but your health data is never included in those requests.
